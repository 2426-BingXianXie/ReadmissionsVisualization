Hospital Readmission Trend

Members: Mei Qi (Amy) Chen, Jenny Zhao, Tianyou Lyu, Eason Xie.

Introduction:

An overall introduction to your project, including the topic and tasks.

Hospital readmission rate is a concern for both healthcare providers and patients. In the majority cases of readmissions, they not only signal potential lapses in patient care continuity but also impose substantial financial burdens on providers and payers. In this project, we will use the “Predicting Hospital Readmissions” dataset from Kaggle to visualize how readmission rates change over time and across different patient groups. By transforming raw numbers into intuitive charts, interactive dashboards, and spatial mappings, we aim to illuminate key patterns and support stakeholders, clinicians, administrators, and policy makers, in identifying areas for improvement and intervention.

Dataset Introduction:

A paragraph to introduce the data, including the size, source and attributes.

Our dataset “Predicting Hospital Readmissions” was retrieved from the public source Kaggle and collected this data to analyze whether or not diabetes is a significant predictor of hospital readmissions. This dataset contains 17 variables and approximately 25,000 rows of data which will be cleaned and processed for missing rows and incomplete information.

Variables Include:
  1. Age
  2. Time in hospital
  3. Number of procedures performed during the hospital stay
  4. Number of laboratory procedures performed during the hospital stay
  5. Number of medications administered during the hospital stay
  6. Outpatient visits in the year before a hospital stay
  7. Inpatient visits in the year before the hospital stay
  8. Visits to the emergency room in the year before the hospital stay
  9. Specialty of the admitting physician
  10. Primary diagnosis
  11. Secondary diagnosis
  12. Additional secondary diagnosis
  13. Glucose serum
  14. A1C level of the patient
  15. Change in the diabetes medication
  16. Whether a diabetes medication was prescribed
  17. Patient was readmitted to the hospital (Yes/No)

Visualization 6: Diagnosis Distribution by Age Group

Key Takeaway: This grouped bar chart reveals how different diagnoses are distributed across age groups. Circulatory conditions show the highest frequency, particularly in the 70-80 age range, which aligns with expected patterns of age-related cardiovascular issues. The visualization allows for easy comparison of diagnosis patterns across the entire patient population age spectrum.

Visualization 7: Medical Specialty Categories with Readmission Rates

Key Finding: This horizontal bar chart identifies which medical specialties face the greatest readmission challenges. Only specialties with 50+ patients are included to ensure statistical reliability, and missing specialty data has been excluded. Specialties above the red average line have elevated readmission rates, suggesting these departments may need targeted interventions such as improved discharge education, enhanced follow-up care, or better care coordination to reduce preventable readmissions.
